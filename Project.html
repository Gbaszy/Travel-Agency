FCT

Abuja, the capital city of Nigeria, was built according to a master plan developed in the 1980s and officially replaced Lagos as the country's capital on December 12, 1991. It lies on land that was originally part of the ancient Habe (Hausa) kingdom of Zazzau (Zaria). The city was mainly built on the grass-covered Chukuku Hills.

Lagos State

Lagos is a major African financial centre and is the economic hub of Lagos State and Nigeria at large1. It is the second largest city in Nigeria and the most populous city in Nigeria and in West Africa, with more than 10 million inhabitants. Lagos is also one of the largest cities in West Africa inhabited by more than 20 million people. The city has grown into a cosmopolitan city over the years. Lagos is surrounded by the Atlantic Ocean.

Akwa Ibom State

Akwa Ibom, state, southeastern Nigeria. Its area formed part of Cross River state until 1987, when Akwa Ibom state was created. Akwa Ibom is bounded by Cross River state on the east, by the Bight of Biafra of the Atlantic Ocean on the south, by Rivers state on the west,and by Abia state on the north.
